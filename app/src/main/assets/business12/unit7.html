MOTIVATION

Concept of Motivation

“Motivation is the act of inspiring and encouraging employees to devote maximum effort to achieve objectives of the organization.”- By Koontz and O’Donnell
“Motivation is a general term applying to the entire class of drivers, desires, needs, wishes and similar forces that induce an individual or a group of people to work.”- By William G. Scott
“Motivation means a process of simulating people to action to achieve desired goals.”

Management is the art of getting things done through others. Manager must have knowledge why and how people act in accordance with organizational requirements. He must also have knowledge to do a particular work with clear vision.

Nature/ Feature of Motivation

“Motivation is a psychological and human aspect. It influences upon of the behavior of subordinates.”
Some features of Motivation
1. PSYCHOLOGICAL PROCESS: - Motivation is a psychological concept. It is the process to gain a desired result by simulating and influencing upon the behavior of subordinates. Subordinates differ in their approach and even two individuals cannot be motivated with the same behavior.
2. CONTINIOUS PROCESS: - As human needs are unlimited, motivation is a continuous process. A satisfied person of today may not be satisfied tomorrow. When one need is satisfied, another need emerges. Thus, motivation is never ending process up to the completion of objectives. It is the responsibility of management to develop new techniques, system and methods to fulfill the changing needs and desires of the workers.
3. CONCENTRATED ON TOTAL INDIVIDUALS: - Individuals are motivated to fulfill their unlimited wants and needs. Here, total individual to be motivated, not the parts of individual. For instance, whenever an individual wishes to wear a pair of shoes, here only feet are not motivated but whole body of the individual is motivated to get shoes.
4. COMPLEX AND UNPRIDICTABLE: - Motivation is complex and unpredictable task. Human wants are unlimited and are changeable according to time and condition.
5. PRESERVE FUNCTION: - Motivation is the pervasive function of all levels of management. Every manager from top to lowest level of management hierarchy is responsible for motivation.

PROCESS OF MOTIVATION

Motivation is the process that starts with a physiological and psychological deficiency or need that activates behavior that is aimed at a goal or incentives.

1. NEEDS: - Needs are created when there is physiological and psychological imbalance in human beings. Needs is the beginning stage of motivation process
2. DRIVES: - Physiological and psychological drives are action oriented. Drives or motives are set up to make easy to fulfill needs. They are the major step of motivation process.
3. INCENTIVES: - Incentives is the end part of motivation cycle. It helps to fulfill need and reduce drive. When people get incentives it helps to restore physical and psychological balance.

Importance of Motivation

“Motivation plays significant role for effectiveness of managerial functions.”

1. LEAD TO PROFITABLE OPERATION: - Motivation is one of the important tools to lead the organization in profitable operation. Motivated workers perform their duties with full responsibility and never want to avoid their responsibility.
2. HIGH LEVEL OF PRODUCTIVITY: - Motivation contributes to develop working efficiency of employees. When employees are actually motivated, they improve their working efficiency.
3. BEST REMEDY FOR RESISTANCE TO CHANGE: - The change of management system and organizational structure is essential to adjust an organization and its business activities according to the changing environment of the society.
4. EFFECTIVE USE OF HUMAN RESOURCES: - Manpower is the main factor of production and is responsible for the best or worst utilization of resources of the organization.
5. SATISFACTION OF EMPLOYEES: - The satisfaction of employees is essential for organizational effectiveness. Motivation helps to satisfy the employees and develop morality among them.

TECNIQUES OF MOTIVATION

“Motivation is the process of encouraging and simulating subordinate to devote maximum for developing organizational efficiency to achieve the organizational objectives.”
1) Financial incentives: - Money is regarded as a symbol of social prestige, recognition and achievement in the materialistic world. People satisfy their higher order needs by money.
2) PARTICIPATION: - Participation refers to involvement of employees in planning and decision making. This helps to fulfill esteem needs of employees participation helps management is cost reduction, satisfy measures, materials handling and operating methods.
3) DELEGATION OF AUTHORITY: - Delegation of authority is assigning certain part of work to subordinates and giving them the required authority to perform the assigned task effectively.
4) JOB SECURITY: - Job security implies that employees would continue on the same job in the sane organization.
5) JOB ENLARGEMENT: -Job enlargement refers to mounting complexity of the job. Employees performs more varied task of the same level. It motivates employees by res reducing monotony of repetitiveness in work.

Types of motivation

1) POSITIVE MOTIVATION: - Positive motivation initiates to offer rewards to persuade employees by providing both monetary and non-monetary benefits.
2) NEGATIVE MOTIVATION: - Negative motivation initiates to hold out some punishment to employees to induce desired behavior.

Maslow’s Need Hierarchy Theory

“Maslow, saw human needs in the form of a hierarchy, ascending from lowest to highest level.”
Abraham H. Maslow, eminent American psychologist, saw human needs in the form of a hierarchy, ascending from lowest to the highest. Maslow, by studying depth about the needs of the people, classified human needs into five stages from basic to self-actualization needs.
 Human needs and motives are complex
 Needs from hierarchy
 Unsatisfied needs are the reasons of motivation
 People seek growth and development
The explanation of Maslow’s need hierarchy theory is as follows.
1) PHYSSIOLOGICAL NEEDS: - Physiological needs are basic needs which involve food, water, clothes, shelter, rest etc.
2) SAFETY/SECURITY NEEDS: - Safety and security needs dwell upon economic and job security, insurance and other protective measures.
3) SOCIAL/AFFILATION NEEDS: - Social needs focus on social values, like love and affection, social acceptance, social status etc.
4) ESTEEM/EGO NEEDS: - Esteem needs emphasize on sell respect and social recognition.
5) SELF-ACTUALIZATION: - At this level individuals seek challenges and creative work for personal growth and advertisement
