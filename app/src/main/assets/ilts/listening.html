1. An Overview of IELTS Listening

2. What is IELTS Listening Test?

Duration and Format:

The Listening test takes around 40 minutes in total. The candidates listen to the recording for 30 minutes and write answers on the Question Paper. When the recording ends, 10 minutes are allowed for candidates to transfer their answers to an Answer Sheet. There are 40 questions in total and each question carries 1 mark. There are four sections with ten questions each. The questions are designed so that the answers appear in the order they are heard in the audio. You will listen to four recordings of native English speakers and then write your answers to a series of questions.

I) Section 1- There is a dialogue between two people set in an everyday social context. For example-
i)a conversation between a travel agent and a customer
ii) a conversation between a flight attendant and a customer

II) Section 2- There is a monologue set in an everyday social context
For example-i)a speech about local facility
ii) a guide explaining the popular travel destination to tourists

III)Section 3- There is a conversation between up to four people set in an educational or training context For example-
i)a university tutor and a student discussing an assignment
ii)Students discussing about their major

IV)Section 4-There is a monologue on an Academic context.
eg- a university lecture, a seminar on wildlife etc

The recordings are heard only once. They include a range of accents, including British, Australian, New Zealand, American and Canadian.


Task Types:
A variety of questions are used chosen from the following task types:
  1. Multiple choice
  2. Short answer questions
  3. Sentence completion
  4. Notes/summary/diagram/flow-chart/table completion
  5. Labeling a diagram with numbered charts
  6. Classification
  7. Matching

Marking and Assessment:

One mark is awarded for each correct answer in the 40-item list. A confidential band score conversion table is produced for each version of the Listening test, which translates score out of 40 into IELTS 9-Band Scale. Scores are reported as a whole or a half band. Care should be taken when writing answers on the Answer Sheet as poor spelling and grammar are penalised.
